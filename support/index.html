Common questions
  • Why is my park showing the wrong score?
    Scores are based on Open-Meteo weather data and community-logged sessions. If the score seems off, log a session (dry or wet) to correct it for other users. You can also adjust your personal tolerance in Settings. Learn how the scoring engine works.
  • How do I delete a location?
    On the Locations tab, swipe left on the location and tap Unsave.
  • How do I delete my data?
    Tap Settings > Reset App to clear all locally stored locations and settings.
  • The app isn't loading weather data.
    Check your internet connection. Weather is fetched from Open-Meteo on each screen load. If the problem persists, send us a feedback email from Settings; it includes logs automatically.
  • A location has incorrect information.
    Swipe left on the location in the Locations tab and tap Flag, or use the flag icon on the Dashboard. Our team reviews flags and corrects bad data.
  • I can't find a park in another city or country when searching.
    Search shows locations within roughly 300 km of your current location. Version 1.1 improved search relevance; if a spot still doesn't appear, it may not have been added yet - use Add Location to contribute it.
  • Which location is used for my widgets?
    Widgets display your favourite locations in the order they appear on the Locations tab. The location at the top of your favourites list is used first. To change which location appears in a widget, reorder your favourites on the Locations tab by dragging them into the order you want.
  • Why does my widget show a different score to the app?
    Widgets currently use the original v1.0 scoring engine because the newer engine has not yet been ported to Swift. This means widget scores may differ from the scores shown inside the app. We plan to ship the Swift migration in version 1.3, after which widgets and the app will use the same engine.

Known bugs

Bugs we're aware of and tracking. Expand for details.

  • Unnecessary failed requests on app load
    When the Dashboard loads before a spot is selected, the app briefly sends requests with an empty identifier, which the server rejects. This does not affect scores, sessions, or any visible behaviour.

    Impact: none visible to users. Causes harmless server-side log noise.
